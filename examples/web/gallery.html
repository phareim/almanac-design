Almanac · Do / Don’t

A gallery of right and wrong

Every rule has a positive and a negative. Read pairs left-to-right.

Hairline, not box
— DO

A scholarly entry

A hairline rule separates the headline from the body. No frame, no shadow.

— DON’T

Generic SaaS card

A boxed card with rounded corners and a drop shadow says “product demo”, not “almanac”.

Accent only at attention
— DO

The accent appears once: — NOTICE here, then nowhere else on the page. Restraint earns the eye.

— DON’T

Some words in a sentence painted like a parade. Multiple accents quietly read as no accent.

Serif body always
— DO

Body in Source Serif 4 (or Georgia fallback). The serif slows the eye to a reading pace, which is the point.

— DON’T

Body in -apple-system / system-ui sans-serif. Looks like every other dashboard. The almanac voice evaporates.

No chrome
— DO

Action as plain text or a tracked monolabel.

— DON’T

Action as a glossy filled button with shadow.

Stars dark-mode only, sparse, static
— DO

Six hand-placed dots, white at low opacity, only when data-theme="dark". No animation. Toggle dark in the corner to see them.

— DON’T

A 300-particle drifting starfield in light mode. The almanac is calm; motion-rich backgrounds pull the eye off the prose.

Measure ≈ 65ch
— DO

A line that ends near the sixty-fifth character returns the eye to the left margin without losing the line. Caps that with max-width: var(--almanac-measure).

— DON’T

A full-viewport-width line that stretches edge to edge regardless of how wide the window is, forcing the eye to swing through twice the necessary distance every line and quietly making the reader slower without telling them why.
